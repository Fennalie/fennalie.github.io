A Journal from the End of Times

September 28, 2012 grant2323 Leave a comment

Our route takes us from the heart of the Zapatista territories in Chiapas, Mexico through to the Maya regions of Guatemala, El Salvador, Honduras and Belize. On our journey we discover the resurgent voice of the Maya people following decades of civil war and centuries of persecution. This story is told through encounters with spiritual guides, environmental activists, shaman, day keepers, Zapatistas, ex-FMLN guerrillas and a intriguing assemblage of characters from modern indigenous life; from hip-hop artists to permaculturists. Our exploration culminates at the COP 16 climate conference in Cancun with the attendance of Bolivia’s Indigenous president Evo Morales speaking to the grassroots movement ‘La Via Campesino’.
The Maya people came to receive global attention with their 2012 prophecies, yet at the time of embarking on our journey little opportunity had been given for these people to be heard. The impetus for this documentary and book was to hear the word of the Maya people; much of what was relayed is the voice of rural communities who are being devastated by the extremes of a changing climate and whose autonomy is continually threatened by the State, the Military and the Church. However the dignity and persistence of Mayan cultural identity is being upheld through their traditional spiritual practice and active worship of ‘Madre Tierra’.

A Journal from the End of Times

This is a chronological photo-blog to accompany 'A Journal from the End of Times.'

THIS BOOK IS NOW AVAILABLE FROM: www.lulu.com

The Journal from the End of Times is a travelogue and contemporary discourse exploring our western lifestyles and examining the current relationship we have with our host planet. Through hope and inspiration gained from time spent with indigenous Maya people in Central America an alternative world view is on offer here. This book examines the impacts of neoliberalism, climate change and a range of social, political and spiritual issues from within Mesoamerica today and globally. This story is told via a travelogue which details a lengthy and perilous journey taken by myself and a film maker to produce a documentary, ‘2012: The Mayan Word’ .
